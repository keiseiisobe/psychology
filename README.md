## Branches of Psychology

Psychology is a diverse and multifaceted field with numerous branches, each focusing on different aspects of human thought, behavior, and emotion. Here's a brief overview of some major branches:

*   **Clinical Psychology:** This is perhaps the most well-known branch, focusing on the assessment, diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of mental disorders. Clinical psychologists often work directly with individuals experiencing psychological distress.

*   **Cognitive Psychology:** Investigates internal mental processes such as problem-solving, memory, learning, attention, perception, and language. It explores how people think and process information.

*   **Developmental Psychology:** Studies how and why human beings change over the course of their life. Initially concerned with infants and children, the field has expanded to include adolescence, adult development, aging, and the entire lifespan.

*   **Social Psychology:** Examines how social influences, social perception, and social interaction impact individual and group behavior. It looks at topics like conformity, obedience, prejudice, and attraction.

*   **Neuropsychology (Biopsychology):** Explores the relationship between brain function and behavior. Neuropsychologists study how the brain and nervous system affect thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, often focusing on the impact of brain injury or neurological conditions.

*   **Counseling Psychology:** Similar to clinical psychology, but often deals with less severe mental health concerns and focuses more on personal and interpersonal functioning across the lifespan, emotional, social, vocational, educational, health-related, developmental, and organizational concerns.

*   **Forensic Psychology:** Applies psychological principles and research methods to the legal and criminal justice system. Forensic psychologists might work with law enforcement, courts, or correctional facilities.

*   **Industrial-Organizational (I-O) Psychology:** Applies psychological theories and principles to organizations and the workplace. I-O psychologists work to improve productivity, job satisfaction, and organizational structure.

*   **Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi:** [Positive Psychology](positive_psychology/mihaly_csikszentmihalyi.md) (Developer of Flow Theory)
*   **John Bowlby:** [Developmental Psychology](developmental_psychology/john_bowlby.md) (Creator of Attachment Theory)
*   **Charles Spearman:** [Psychometrics](psychometrics/charles_spearman.md) (Two-factor theory of intelligence and factor analysis)
